The Night of the Three Giants

When the sky cracked open with light, three giants stepped out — not to rule, but to remind us who watches the horizon.

The Watcher of Stone

He guarded the silence between the cliffs. When I asked what he sought, he said, “Patience is taller than any tower.”

The Listener of Wind

Every breath he took stirred the clouds. He said nothing, but the air itself began to sing through the valleys.

The Keeper of Steps

His footprints glowed faintly in the dark. “Keep moving,” he said, “or the earth forgets your name.”
